# Best Model for Multi-Agent Systems

## Executive Summary
This report evaluates the latest AI models that could potentially replace GPT-4o in multi-agent systems as of 2026. The focus is on models that offer advanced capabilities and integration potential for complex environments.

## Key Findings

### Nemotron 3 by NVIDIA
- **Description**: A large language model designed for both reasoning and non-reasoning tasks, part of the Nemotron model family.
- **Advantages**: Advanced training techniques like SFT, RLVR, and RLHF; suitable for environments like NeMo Gym.
- **Disadvantages**: Requires significant computational resources.
- **Source**: [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/blog/nvidia/nemotron-3-nano-efficient-open-intelligent-models)

### GPT-5 by OpenAI
- **Description**: Released in March 2026, featuring a novel router architecture and native multimodal training.
- **Advantages**: Designed for coding and interactive app creation.
- **Disadvantages**: Issues with router misfires and personality depth.
- **Source**: [Popular AI Tools](https://popularaitools.ai/blog/gpt-5-review-2026)

### Claude 3 by Anthropic
- **Description**: Includes sophisticated vision capabilities, processing various visual formats.
- **Advantages**: Claude 3.5 Sonnet is a standout model.
- **Disadvantages**: Limited information on integration specifics.
- **Source**: [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-3-family)

## Comparison with GPT-4o
- **GPT-4o**: Known for its multimodal capabilities integrating text, vision, and audio.
- **Nemotron 3**: Offers advanced reasoning capabilities, potentially more suitable for environments requiring high-level reasoning.
- **GPT-5**: Provides enhanced coding and app creation features, which may be beneficial for systems requiring dynamic content generation.
- **Claude 3**: Offers advanced vision processing, which could be advantageous for systems needing robust visual data handling.

## Recommendation
For multi-agent systems requiring advanced reasoning and integration capabilities, **Nemotron 3** is recommended due to its comprehensive training techniques and adaptability to complex environments. However, for systems focused on coding and app development, **GPT-5** may be more suitable.
